Pork Tamales

Tamales are a traditional Mesoamerican recipe that uses masa for dough and a corn husk to wrap it. Do not eat the corn husk, it is not edible. This recipe will teach you how to make pork tamales using our original salsa recipe. If you see any ingredients you don't own, just click the plus and it will be added to the cart. Prep Time: 45 minutes

Ingredients:

  • pork shoulder(s) (3.5 lbs)
  • bay leaves
  • tsp(s) of coarse kosher salt (regular salt can be used if necessary)
  • + shakes of pepper
  • dashes of cumin
  • 1 bag of corn husks
  • can(s) of italian diced tomatoes
  • tbsp(s) of salt + ~ of salt
  • dashe(s) of garlic powder
  • - dried red chillies (depends on how spicy you want it)
  • tbsp(s) of olive oil
  • cups of masa (4.41 lb bag = 16 cups)
  • cup(s) of crisco
  • ~ cups of hot water

Instructions:

  1. Cooking Meat
    1. Put pork in slow cooker and fill w/ water until meat is just barely submerged (if you fill too much it might boil over)
    2. Add bay leaves, kosher salt, shakes of pepper and cumin
    3. Cook on high for about 5 hours or on low for about 7 hours overnight - pork will be ready when it can easily fall off the bone
  2. Use tongs to pull the pork out and place it in a large bowl
  3. Use forks to pull off fat areas and discard them
  4. Begin using forks to shred pork by piercing a section with one and stroking the meat into little pieces with the other one, then set aside
  5. Fill a sink with hot water and place about separated corn husks (you might need more or less later on) in the water with a bowl on top to hold them down
  6. Make Salsa
    1. Empty can of tomatoes in blender and add of salt, shake(s) of pepper and garlic powder
    2. Fill empty tomato can with water and add to blender
    3. Break the tops of chiles off, rinse our seeds (not necessary to get rid of all seeds) and add to blender
    4. Blend for about 30 seconds or until mixture is smooth and chunkless, set aside
  7. Heat up a large pan and add the olive oil
  8. Once it’s heated up, add the chile and wait for bubbles to appear to indicate that it is boiling
  9. Put the meat in the pan and once it starts boiling again, set to a low heat to keep it warm
  10. Make the Maseca
    1. In a large bowl add together the masa, crisco and tbsp(s) of salt
    2. Add 1/4 cup of hot water to the maseca mixture at a time while mixing with your hands until it’s a smooth and firm mixture that doesn’t crumble - this should add up to around cups of water being added
  11. Assembling the Tamale
    1. Take out a corn husks and dry off with a towel
    2. Use your palms to press down a thinish layer of maseca all across the corn husk except for the lower two inches (see in picture below) - you could also use a wet spoon to spread the maseca if you don’t want to use your hand
    3. Take some meat from the pot and place an excessive amount in the center of the tamale
    4. Wrap the tamale by rolling it up and flipping the lowest part of the cornhusk up
    5. Place the tamale face down in a double broiler
    6. Repeat steps until you run out of meat and as you fill up the broiler, place tamales in a tic tac toe pattern that has two lying vertical and another two lying horizontal on top - make sure that there is always a hole in the center of this pattern
  12. Cooking Tamales
    1. Fill pot about ⅓ of the way with water
    2. Cook on a medium flame for about 60-90 minutes and make sure to have a small (but not too large) opening for the steam to exit from
    3. You can check if they are ready by pulling one out and opening up the corn husk, if the insides stick to the husk, it isn’t ready yet
    4. Once they are ready bring to the sink to drain any left over water and let cool for a while
  13. Enjoy and refrigerate any left overs

Portion Size:

*This portion size makes about tamales*

Pro Tip

If you have a sweet tooth you can always replace the pork insides with other ingredients like sugar and raisins or almonds. There isn't really a limit on what you can put inside as long as it can be cooked. Since these treats will be incorperated in the maseca, rather than being used as a filling, make sure to mix them in with the maseca as you prepare them.

Enjoy!
