Chicken Enchiladas

Chicken enchiladas are a Mexican dish that wrap chicken in tortillas and cover with a sauce. Our version of enchiladas layers the meat in tortillas and uses our own personal salsa recipe to add a zing of flavor. Ingredients:

  • Boneless, Skinless Chicken Breasts
  • ~ Corn Tortillas (will vary depending on size of pan)
  • ~ cup(s) of fiesta or mexican shredded cheese
  • onion(s) diced up
  • Olive or vegetable oil (amount will vary)
  • can(s) of italian diced tomatoes
  • - dried red chilies
  • tsp(s) of salt
  • pinch(es) of pepper
  • dash(es) of garlic

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375 F
  2. Lather chicken in oil and cook in oven for about 25-30 minutes or until internal temp is at least 165 F
  3. Once done, shred the chicken in a bowl and set aside
  4. Make the Salsa
    1. Empty can of tomatoes in blender
    2. Rinse chillies and add to blender
    3. Season w/ salt, pepper and garlic powder
    4. Fill empty tomato can with water equal to half the amount of tomatoes you used and add to blender (to get the last of the tomato stuff out and add more as needed)
    5. Blend for about 30-45 seconds or until mixture is smooth: w/o chunks
  5. Pour all of salsa in frying pan - keep it on a low simmer (no boiling)
  6. While it's warming, warm the tortillas in microwave so they are pliable
  7. Set another small frying pan on a burner and fill it w/ the oil of your choice so that the bottom is coated enough to submerge the tortillas - low/medium heat
  8. Assemble the Enchilada
    1. Take 1 tortilla and place it in the pan with oil, use a slotted spatula to flip it and let it sit for about 5-8 seconds
    2. Pick up the tortilla w/ the spatula and grab a new tortilla on the palm of your other hand, press them together in order to pad the grease onto the new one
    3. Move the soaked tortilla to the salsa on the other burner, let it sit in salsa for about 2-3 seconds - if it’s in there too long it will start to fall apart
    4. Meanwhile put the tortilla from your other hand in the oil
    5. Take the tortilla out, no need to pad off excess salsa, and place it on the bottom layer of the pan
    6. Repeat steps ii.-v. until the bottom of the pan is covered while tortillas are slightly overlapping
    7. Repeat w/ more tortillas (usually about 6) until the bottom of the pan is covered (slightly overlapping)
    8. Spread a light layer of shredded chicken, onion and cheese on top
    9. Repeat steps vii.-viii. one more time and then add a final layer of 6 tortillas on top
    10. Cover this with cheese and onion
    11. Pour whatever salsa is left from the pan on top (shouldn’t be much left)
  9. Set oven to high broil and stick it in for about 3-5 minutes so the cheese can melt and the dish can warm up
  10. Remove and let it cool for a couple of minutes before serving

Portion Size:

*This portion size makes about enchiladas depending on how big you cut the slices*

Pro Tip

Remove the seeds from the chiles before blending to reduce the spiciness of the salsa.

Enjoy!
